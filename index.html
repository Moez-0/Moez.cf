Engolfed in the spectrum of living and trying to live

As a human being, having a philosophy is unavoidable. Your only choice is whether to consciously and rationally develop this philosophy through disciplined thought and logical deliberation, or to let your subconscious piece together a disorganized collection of unwarranted conclusions, false generalizations, contradictions, slogans, and fears. This haphazard mix, integrated by your subconscious, can become a heavy burden of self-doubt, holding you back like a ball and chain where your mind's wings should be.

Alexithymia, derived from Greek, translates to "no words for emotions." It describes a condition where individuals struggle to identify, express, and understand their own emotions. The term encompasses a spectrum of emotional awareness, with some individuals being completely unaware of their feelings, while others may have a limited emotional vocabulary, making emotional expression and understanding a complex and nuanced experience.

Resentment is a conscious act of holding onto feelings of bitterness and anger towards someone or something perceived as having wronged or unfairly treated you. Unlike fleeting feelings of anger, resentment is nurtured and sustained over time. It involves a deliberate choice to revisit and relive the perceived offense, reinforcing negative emotions and fostering a cycle of blame and victimhood. This persistent state of mind can erode mental and emotional well-being, affecting one's ability to move forward and find peace. Understanding that resentment is a conscious act can empower individuals to actively work on letting go of these harmful emotions and embrace forgiveness and healing.

"Starve the ego, feed the soul" is a phrase that encourages focusing on inner growth and spiritual fulfillment rather than superficial desires and self-centeredness. To starve the ego means to let go of the need for external validation, pride, and selfish ambitions. It involves quieting the inner critic and not being driven by fear, envy, or the desire for control and power. On the other hand, feeding the soul involves nurturing your true self through acts of kindness, mindfulness, and personal growth. It emphasizes cultivating inner peace, compassion, and a deeper understanding of oneself and others. By prioritizing soul-nourishing activities over ego-driven pursuits, one can achieve a more balanced, fulfilling, and meaningful life.

"Memento mori" is a Latin phrase meaning "remember that you must die." It serves as a reminder of the inevitability of death, encouraging individuals to reflect on the transient nature of life. This contemplation is intended to inspire humility, mindfulness, and a focus on what truly matters. By keeping the reality of mortality in mind, one can prioritize meaningful pursuits, cultivate gratitude, and live more fully in the present moment. The concept of "memento mori" has been represented in various forms of art, literature, and philosophy throughout history, serving as a poignant reminder to live with intention and purpose.
